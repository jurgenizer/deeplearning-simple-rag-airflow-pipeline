## Seminal articles
"Technology Affordances and Constraints in Management Theory." Organizational Sciences, 25(1), 165-186.  By Ann Majchrzak (University of Southern California - Marshall School of Business) and M. Lynne Markus (Bentley University)

Although not labeled as "TACT," several earlier works significantly influenced its development by Majchrzak and Markus:
Gibson, J. J. (1979). The theory of affordances. In The people, place, and space reader (pp. 56-60). Routledge.
[Originated the concept of “affordances” in ecological psychology]

Norman, D. A. (1988). The psychology of everyday things. Basic books.
[Adapted affordances to human-computer interaction, emphasizing perceived affordances]

Orlikowski, W. J. (1992). The duality of technology: Rethinking the concept of technology in organizations. Organization science, 3(3), 398-427.
[Influential in showing how technology and human action are mutually constitutive]

Zammuto, R. F., Griffith, T. L., Majchrzak, A., Dougherty, D. J., & Faraj, S. (2007). Information technology and the changing fabric of organization. Organization science, 18(5), 749-762.
[A critical precursor, framing the groundwork for TACT by addressing affordances explicitly.]

Markus, M. L., & Silver, M. S. (2008). A foundation for the study of IT effects: A new look at DeSanctis and Poole's concepts of structural features and spirit. Journal of the Association for Information systems, 9(10), 5.
[Bridged sociotechnical perspectives with a focus on materiality and use]
