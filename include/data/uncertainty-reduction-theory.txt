# Uncertainty Reduction Theory

## Concise description of theory
Uncertainty Reduction Theory (URT; Berger 1979; Berger and Calabrese 1975) emphasizes that during initial interactions, individuals focus primarily on reducing uncertainty about their own and their partner’s behavior. The theory outlines three main strategies people use to gather information and enhance predictability: passive, active, and interactive. Passive strategies involve discreetly observing others to gain insights. Active strategies include seeking information indirectly through third parties or by altering the target’s environment to provoke responses. Interactive strategies involve direct communication, such as asking questions or sharing personal information, to elicit responses.

## Originating author(s)
Charles R. Berger, and Richard J. Calabrese
