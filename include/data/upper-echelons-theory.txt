# Upper Echelons Theory

## Concise description of theory
The upper echelons theory is a management theory published by Donald C. Hambrick and Phyllis A. Mason in 1984. It states that organizational outcomes are partially predicted by managerial background characteristics of the top-level management team.

Key Concepts
Managerial Characteristics is the demographic and psychological attributes of executives guide their perceptions, judgments, and decisions. Top Management Team (TMT) takes the strategic decisions. Strategic Choices and Outcomes are the organizational strategies, performance, and adaptability are direct reflections of the upper echelon’s perspectives.

Applications:

Executive Recruitment, Strategic Forecasting, Comparative Analysis

## Originating author(s)
Donald C. Hambrick and Phyllis Mason (1984)
